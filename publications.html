Published Papers

Planktonic foraminifera test size dictated by conditions in life and postmortem

Suárez-Ibarra, J.Y., Vieira, I., Frozza, C.F., Chaabane, S., Palhano, P.L., Kovář, V., Chalk, T.B., Anjos-Zerfass, G.S., de Garidel-Thoron, T., Holcová, K., Pivel, M.A.G. (2025)

Journal of Foraminiferal Research, 55(3): 320–335

Impact Factor: 0.7 | Citations: 0

Identified critical caveats on size-dependent proxy reconstructions. The positive and significant correlation between carbonate preservation and tests size suggests that post-mortem processes have the potential to mask palaeoecological signals in planktonic foraminifera through terminal chamber(s) loss.

Where it's worth it: frequency and spatial distribution of bioerosional drill holes in planktonic foraminifera reveal different strategies in site selectivity

Frozza, C.F., Suárez-Ibarra, J.Y., Matias, R., Coimbra, J., Pivel, M.A.G. (2024)

Paleobiology, 50(4): 592–607

Impact Factor: 2.7 | Citations: 0

First spatial mapping of bioerosion hotspots on planktonic foraminifera, revealing spinose species are more vulnerable to multiple drills than the non-spinose ones. Demonstrated that bioeroders strategically target high cost–benefit regions, with test architecture driving species-specific susceptibility patterns.

Surface fertilisation and organic matter delivery enhanced carbonate dissolution in the western South Atlantic

Suárez-Ibarra, J.Y., Freire, T.M., Frozza, C.F., Pinho, T.M., Petró, S.M., Dias, B., Chalk, T., Chaabane, S., Srivastava, M., Costa, K., Toledo, F., de Garidel-Thoron, T., Coimbra, J.C., Pivel, M.A.G. (2023)

Frontiers in Ecology and Evolution, 11:1238334

Impact Factor: 2.6 | Citations: 5

Documented obliquity-controlled glacial fertilisation mechanisms (dust/upwelling) in the western South Atlantic using foraminiferal proxies, linking surface productivity and deep carbonate dissolution through enhanced labile carbon export.

Calcium carbonate dissolution triggered by high productivity during the last glacial-deglacial interval at the deep western South Atlantic

Suárez-Ibarra, J.Y., Frozza, C.F., Palhano, P.L., Petró, S.M., Weinkauf, M.F.G., Pivel, M.A.G. (2022)

Frontiers in Earth Science, 10: 830984

Impact Factor: 2.0 | Citations: 12

Through micropalaeontologic and sedimentologic proxies we recognised enhanced glacial upwelling (insolation-driven) in the western South Atlantic. This process caused carbonate dissolution above the lysocline through the remineralisation of exported organic matter and release of CO₂.

Fragment or broken? Improving the planktonic foraminifera fragmentation assessment

Suárez-Ibarra, J.Y., Frozza, C.F., Petró, S.M., Pivel, M.A.G. (2021)

PALAIOS, 36(4): 165–172

Impact Factor: 1.5 | Citations: 11

We proposed the Fragmentation Intensity (FI), the first image-analysis-based metric that integrates morphometric parameters (area/perimeter) of planktonic foraminifera fragments to objectively quantify dissolution effects, replacing more subjective traditional indices.

Time-spatial boundaries of bioecozonations (planktonic foraminifera) in the latest Quaternary: a case study from the western South Atlantic

Suárez-Ibarra, J.Y., Petró, S.M., Frozza, C.F., Freire, T.M., Portilho-Ramos, R.C., Pivel, M.A.G. (2021)

Revue de Micropaléontologie, 73:100554

Impact Factor: 1.0 | Citations: 3

Using a strongly ¹⁴C calibrated core, we dated key bioevents in the western South Atlantic, documenting, for the first time, an asynchronous bioevent with around 20 kyr delay, already documented for the equatorial Atlantic and Caribbean, and establishing new regional markers.

Quaternary Proboscidean (Mammalia) remains of the UIS geological museum, Colombia

Suárez-Ibarra, J.Y., Cardoso, G., Asevedo, L., França, L.D.M., Dantas, M.A.T., Cruz-Guevara, L.E., Rojas-Mantilla, A.F., Ribeiro, A.M. (2021)

Impact Factor: 0.6 | Citations: 6

During my BSc, I built a research bridge between Colombian and Brazilian researchers to study gomphothere fossil remains found more than 60 years ago, resulting in this scientific publication. Bioerosion on late Quaternary planktonic foraminifera related to paleoproductivity in the Western South Atlantic

Frozza, C.F., Pivel, M.A.G., Suárez-Ibarra, J.Y., Ritter, M.N., Coimbra, J.C. (2020)

Paleoceanography and Paleoclimatology, 35(8): e2020PA003865

Impact Factor: 3.2 | Citations: 19

This study pioneers on the use of drill holes on planktonic foraminifera tests as a novel palaeoceanographic proxy, quantifying previously overlooked predation patterns. Higher bioerosion rates occurred during high productivity and low temperature intervals.
